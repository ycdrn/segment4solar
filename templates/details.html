Calculation Method

The potential annual energy yield (\( E_y \)) of a photovoltaic (PV) system installed on a building facade is calculated using the following equation:

\[ E_y = A \times H \times \eta \times (1 - L) \]

Where:

  • A: Available facade area for PV installation (m²)
  • H: Annual global irradiance on the facade (kWh/m²/year)
  • \(\eta\): System efficiency based on PV technology (%)
  • L: System losses (%)

This equation estimates the total annual energy production expected from the photovoltaic installation by considering the available installation area estimated with the help of Segment Anything [1], local irradiance data retrieved from PVGIS [2], system efficiency, and inherent system losses. The following are considered constant in the calculations:

Parameter Value Description
System Losses (\( L \)) 0.14 Accounts for 14% energy losses due to factors like shading, temperature effects, inverter efficiency, and wiring losses.
Efficiency (\( \eta \)) - Monocrystalline 0.20 Represents the efficiency of monocrystalline PV modules in converting sunlight to electricity.
Efficiency (\( \eta \)) - Polycrystalline 0.15 Represents the efficiency of polycrystalline PV modules in converting sunlight to electricity.
Efficiency (\( \eta \)) - Thin Film 0.10 Represents the efficiency of thin-film PV modules in converting sunlight to electricity.

For a detailed explanation of the improvements in the estimated energy yield with the methodologies used in our analysis tool, please refer to our conference paper [3].
